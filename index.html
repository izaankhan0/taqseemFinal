Our Story

The Taqseem Foundation is a father-daughter duo NGO founded in 2021 to provide education to less privileged children. We currently serve 800 students across 4 schools, offering classes from Montessori to grade 9th. In addition to our current programs, we are looking to add vocational training programs for women in these areas. We believe that education is the key to breaking the cycle of poverty, and we are committed to empowering both children and women through education and Vocational education and training (VET) training.

Our goal is to provide women with the necessary resources to enhance their skills to succeed in the local job market, helping them build a brighter future for themselves and their families. In addition, we provide daily lunches to our students, ensuring they have access to healthy meals. We believe that proper nutrition is crucial for a child's development and academic success. We rely on the support of like-minded people and partners to continue our work, and we are dedicated to making a difference in the lives of those we serve.

Mission

To empower less privileged children and women through education and enabling talent trainings, providing them with resources they need to enhance their skills to break the cycle of poverty and build a brighter future for themselves and their communities.

Vision

We are committed to supporting every child and woman within our capacity, providing access to education, nutritious meals and a nurturing environment to enable them to reach their full potential and contribute to a brighter future for all.

Values

Our organization's core values include empowering through education, ensuring inclusivity and integrity, practicing compassion, striving for excellence, fostering collaboration, respecting diversity, and prioritizing sustainability.
